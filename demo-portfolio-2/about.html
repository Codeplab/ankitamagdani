My Name is Ankita Magdani & This Is My Story

Childhood and Family

I grew up in India, surrounded by the rich traditions of yoga, meditation, and hypnosis. My mom is a Reiki Master, and my uncles are Feng Shui and Yoga Masters. As a kid, I didn’t really appreciate any of this. I actually thought it was all a bit of a scam!

When I was six, my family experienced a traumatic event—a road accident that took my father’s life. My mom had to “move on” quickly to support my sister and me. She got a job at a bank, earning just 2000 rupees a month (about $50 back then). We never really talked about our loss, which left us with a lot of unprocessed grief.

Mom was super strict, especially about who we talked to. I became the “rebel” in the family, struggling in school and feeling like a lost cause. But then I discovered psychology, and it changed everything.

Early Career Dreams & Journey

I always wanted to be a flight attendant and see the world. But with no concept of pocket money, it was tough. My mom gave me 50 rupees every four days, which had to cover fuel for my scooter and maybe a snack if anything was left.

I fell in love with psychology in college and started tutoring my peers for some extra cash. At 16, I began doing odd jobs like making greeting cards and henna designs. By 17, I got a job as an HR consultant, and then at 18, I became a flight attendant with Jet Airways. I promised my mom I’d finish college, and I did!

Family Dynamics & Trauma

As we grew, my mom got more into religion and societal norms, and I drifted further apart. I found love and support outside, which was tough with family restrictions. Life got really complicated juggling my job, studies, and trying to find myself.

Despite being financially independent, I was still accountable for every penny I spent and every person I spoke to. When Mom found out about my second boyfriend, she forced me to marry him. I changed my religion, name, and lifestyle, thinking I was starting fresh.

My Career Journey

My time as a flight attendant was a pivotal moment in my life. It taught me discipline, adaptability, and the joy of connecting with people from all walks of life. This experience laid the foundation for my passion for personal growth and helping others.

Overcoming Challenges

Married life was a nightmare—abusive in every way. After five years, a miscarriage, and finding out about my husband’s affair, I had enough. My relationship with Mom was bitter; I blamed her for my situation.

In 2012, Emirates Airlines in Dubai had an opening. My husband convinced me to apply so he could try again later. I took the job and moved to Dubai, hitting rock bottom again with my husband’s harassment. But then I found the courage to end the marriage.

By 2013, I was truly free and focused on my career and travels. I met a wonderful man, got promoted at Emirates, and thought I was living the dream. But then anxiety and depression hit hard.

Success and Healing

I started therapy, but real healing began when I enrolled in a diploma in Clinical Hypnotherapy and Psychotherapy. I realized I was carrying unprocessed grief from childhood, resentment towards my mom, and trauma from my marriage.

In 2019, I changed careers and became a mental health therapist. I’ve helped over 1000 clients overcome anxiety, depression, grief, and more. I’m proud of the lives I’ve touched and the impact I’ve made.

Vision: To help individuals shift their perception of life so they can live with integrity, meaning, freedom, and choice.
